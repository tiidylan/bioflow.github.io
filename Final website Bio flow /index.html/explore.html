Understanding Hydroponics

What is hydroponic farming?

Hydroponics is a method of growing plants without soil. Instead, plants are grown in nutrient-rich water solutions, allowing roots to absorb minerals directly. This revolutionary approach to agriculture produces faster growth, higher yields, and uses significantly less water than traditional farming.

In Mauritius, where arable land is limited and the tropical climate presents unique challenges, hydroponics offers an innovative solution. It allows farmers and home growers to produce fresh, pesticide-free vegetables and herbs year-round, regardless of weather conditions or soil quality.

From leafy greens and herbs to tomatoes, strawberries, and peppers — hydroponics makes it possible to grow a wide variety of crops in controlled environments, ensuring consistent quality and nutrition.

The Mauritian Context

Why hydroponics matters
for Mauritius

🏝️

Limited Arable Land

Mauritius has only about 44,000 hectares of agricultural land, and this is shrinking due to urbanisation. Hydroponics allows vertical and indoor farming, maximising output per square metre.

🌊

Water Scarcity

Despite being an island, Mauritius faces periodic water shortages. Hydroponic systems use up to 90% less water than traditional agriculture by recirculating nutrient solutions.

🛳️

Food Import Dependency

Mauritius imports approximately 75% of its food. Local hydroponic production reduces dependency on imports, ensures fresher produce, and strengthens food security for the island.

🌀

Cyclone Resilience

Tropical cyclones regularly damage open-field crops. Indoor and greenhouse hydroponic systems are protected from extreme weather, ensuring uninterrupted food production.

🧑‍🌾

Youth Employment

Hydroponics offers a modern, tech-driven approach to agriculture that appeals to younger Mauritians, creating new career opportunities in agri-tech and sustainable farming.

🌿

Pesticide-Free Produce

Controlled hydroponic environments significantly reduce the need for pesticides and herbicides, producing cleaner, healthier food for Mauritian families.

Hydroponic Systems

Popular systems used
in Mauritius

Most Popular

Nutrient Film Technique (NFT)

A thin film of nutrient solution continuously flows over the plant roots in shallow channels. This is the most widely used system in Mauritius due to its efficiency and simplicity.

NFT systems are ideal for growing lettuce, pak choi, basil, and other leafy greens — crops that are in high demand across the island's hotels, restaurants, and supermarkets.

Low water usage Fast growth Easy maintenance Scalable
Best for Beginners

Deep Water Culture (DWC)

Plants are suspended in net pots with their roots submerged in oxygenated, nutrient-rich water. Air pumps keep the solution aerated, promoting rapid root development and vigorous growth.

DWC is perfect for home growers in Mauritius who want to start small. It's affordable, easy to set up, and produces excellent results with herbs like basil, mint, and coriander.

Simple setup Affordable Great for herbs Beginner friendly
Space Saver

Vertical Tower Gardens

Plants grow in stacked layers on vertical towers, with nutrient solution dripping from top to bottom. This system maximises growing space — perfect for Mauritius where land is at a premium.

Tower gardens can grow up to 20 plants in just one square metre of floor space. They're ideal for apartments, balconies, rooftops, and small urban spaces across Port Louis, Curepipe, and Quatre Bornes.

The Advantages

Benefits of hydroponic
farming in Mauritius

💧

Water Conservation

Recirculating systems use up to 90% less water — critical for an island that experiences seasonal droughts and water rationing.

📈

Higher Yields

Controlled environments produce 3-10 times more food per square metre compared to traditional open-field farming in Mauritius.

🏠

Grow Anywhere

From a small apartment in Port Louis to a rooftop in Flic en Flac — hydroponics works in any space, indoors or outdoors.

🍅

Fresher, Healthier Food

Harvest minutes before eating. No long supply chains, no cold storage — just fresh, nutrient-dense produce for your family.

🌍

Reduced Carbon Footprint

Less transportation, no heavy machinery, and minimal packaging. Growing locally in Mauritius means fewer food miles and lower emissions.

💰

Economic Opportunity

Start a micro-farm with minimal investment. Many Mauritians are turning hydroponic growing into profitable small businesses.
